Injury Modification Guide

Exercise modifications for common client complaints. Trainers modify — not diagnose.

Scope of practice: Stop training and advise physician/PT consult if client reports sharp, radiating, sudden-onset, or worsening pain.
Lower Body

Knee Pain

Avoid Deep knee flexion, plyometrics, forward-knee lunges

Modify To
  • Box squat
  • Leg press (foot position high)
  • Step-ups (6-8 inch box)
  • Romanian deadlift
Lower Body

Low Back Pain

Avoid Loaded spinal flexion, heavy deadlifts, sit-ups

Modify To
  • Bird dog
  • Dead bug progressions
  • Glute bridges
  • Pallof press
Upper Body

Shoulder Impingement

Avoid Overhead press, upright rows, behind-neck movements

Modify To
  • Landmine press
  • Cable face pulls
  • Band external rotation
  • Neutral-grip DB press
Lower Body

Plantar Fasciitis

Avoid Running, jumping, high-impact cardio

Modify To
  • Seated stationary bike
  • Swimming
  • Upper body or seated resistance
  • Calf stretching 3x30s each session

Nutrition Reference

Evidence-based nutrition guidance within trainer scope of practice. Refer detailed planning to a Registered Dietitian.

Fat Loss

Weight Loss Macros

  • Protein: 1.6-2.2 g/kg BW
  • Carbs: 3-5 g/kg
  • Fat: 0.8-1.2 g/kg
  • Deficit: 300-500 kcal below TDEE
Muscle Gain

Hypertrophy Macros

  • Protein: 1.6-2.2 g/kg BW
  • Carbs: 5-7 g/kg
  • Fat: 1.0-1.5 g/kg
  • Surplus: 200-400 kcal above TDEE
Supplements

Evidence-Based Only

  • Creatine: 3-5g/day
  • Whey protein: Complete fast-digesting source
  • Caffeine: 3-6 mg/kg, 30-60 min pre-exercise
  • Vitamin D3: 1000-2000 IU/day
  • Omega-3: 1-3g/day
GLP-1 Clients

AOM Nutrition Priority

  • Protein: 1.8-2.4 g/kg — higher due to appetite suppression
  • Protein-first eating at every meal
  • 4-5 small meals — large meals often not tolerable
  • Whey/casein shakes when appetite too low
  • Refer to RD for meal planning

Recovery Methods

Evidence-based recovery modalities from EPI Phase 2.

Hydrotherapy

Cold Water Immersion

Strong Evidence
  • Protocol: 1 x 5 min at 10C (50F)
  • Waist to shoulder immersion depth
  • Reduces DOMS, speeds recovery
Hydrotherapy

Contrast Water Therapy

Strong Evidence
  • Protocol: 4 min at 40C + 2 min at 10C x 2 rounds
  • Always finish cold
  • Most popular in elite sport settings
Active Recovery

Low-Intensity Active Recovery

Moderate Evidence
  • Protocol: Stationary bike 1 x 15 min at 50-60% MaxHR
  • Most common recovery method used by athletes
Compression

Compression Garments

Moderate Evidence
  • Wear immediately after training for remainder of day
  • Reduces muscle soreness and CK levels

Strength and Power

EPI Phase 2 content — neuromuscular system, hypertrophy, force-velocity curve.

Neuromuscular

Motor Unit Recruitment

  • Size Principle: CNS recruits smallest (Type I) units first
  • Two factors: level of force and rate of force development (RFD)
  • Lifting lighter loads fast recruits most motor units at loads as low as 33% 1RM
Hypertrophy

3 Mechanisms of Muscle Growth

  • 1. Mechanical Tension: Primary driver
  • 2. Metabolic Stress: The pump — cell swelling and hormonal response
  • 3. Muscle Damage (EIMD): Eccentric actions cause most damage
Power

Complex Training and PAP

  • Pair heavy loaded exercise with plyometric exercise
  • Intracomplex rest: 1-4 minutes
  • Requires more than 2 years RT experience
  • Example: Back Squat 3x3 at 90% then CMJ 3x4

Warmup Protocols

OPT model-aligned warmup libraries. A proper warmup reduces injury risk and primes the nervous system.

Lower Body Day

Dynamic Warmup — Legs/Hips

  • Foam roll: quads, IT band, glutes, calves (60 sec each)
  • Hip flexor stretch: 30 sec per side
  • Glute bridges: 2x15
  • Lateral band walks: 2x15 per direction
  • Leg swings: 2x10 per leg
  • Bodyweight squats: 2x10
Upper Body Day

Dynamic Warmup — Push/Press

  • Foam roll: thoracic spine, lats, pecs (60 sec each)
  • Band pull-aparts: 2x15
  • Y-T-W raises (light): 2x10 each
  • Scapular pushups: 2x10
  • External rotation with band: 2x12 per arm
Older Adults 55+

Low-Impact Warmup Protocol

  • 5-8 min recumbent bike (very low intensity)
  • Gentle foam rolling: 4-5 min
  • Ankle circles and wrist circles: 10 each direction
  • Seated hip flexor marches: 15 reps
  • Standing heel raises (with support): 2x15
  • Bodyweight mini squats (chair assist): 2x10

Special Populations

Programming considerations for the most common client populations.

Priority — Muscle PreservationWithout resistance training, 25-40% of weight lost is lean muscle mass. Resistance training is the intervention. Training
  • Resistance training: 3-4 days/week minimum
  • Limit cardio to 2-3 days/week
  • Check in on nausea and energy before every session
  • Protein target: 1.8-2.4 g/kg BW
Primary GoalsFunctional independence, fall prevention, bone density, cardiovascular health. Programming
  • Frequency: 2-3 days resistance plus 2-3 days moderate cardio
  • Intensity: Start 40-60% 1RM
  • Rest: 90-120 sec
  • Tempo: Controlled (2-2-2) — no ballistic movements
  • Balance: Include in every session
Return Timeline
  • Week 1-2: OPT Phase 1 only — stability, proprioception, no loading
  • Week 3-4: Bodyweight plus minimal resistance, pain-free ROM only
  • Week 5-8: Add 5-10% load weekly if pain less than 3/10

Quick Reference

Fast-access cards for mid-session lookups.

FITT-VP

Programming Principle

  • Frequency — how often per week
  • Intensity — % 1RM, HR zone, or RPE
  • Time — session duration
  • Type — resistance, cardio, flexibility
  • Volume — total sets x reps x load
  • Progression — advance every 4-6 weeks
Heart Rate Zones

Cardio Intensity Guide

  • Zone 1 (50-60% MHR): Recovery, warm-up
  • Zone 2 (60-70% MHR): Fat oxidation, aerobic base
  • Zone 3 (70-80% MHR): Aerobic capacity
  • Zone 4 (80-90% MHR): Anaerobic threshold
  • Zone 5 (90-100% MHR): Max effort / HIIT peaks
RPE Scale

Perceived Exertion

  • 1-2: Very easy — rest
  • 3-4: Light — full conversation
  • 5-6: Moderate — short sentences
  • 7-8: Hard — difficult to speak
  • 9: Very hard — a few words only
  • 10: Maximum — cannot speak
NASM CEx

Corrective Exercise Sequence

  • 1. Inhibit: SMR on overactive muscles
  • 2. Lengthen: Static stretch overactive muscles
  • 3. Activate: Isolate underactive muscles
  • 4. Integrate: Compound movement pattern
